SAFETY SIGNS SYMBOLS AND LABELS

Safety signs, symbols and labels are used to show warnings about dangers that are related to the use of agricultural inputs. These signs minimize hazards in agricultural production.

Examples of safety signs, symbols and labels are;

1. Skull and Crossbones

It is a symbol made up of human skull with two long bones crossed below it. It is a warning symbol of poisonous substances and danger. It is best applied with gloves and masks on for safety.

2. Chemical Warning Labels

A chemical warning label is put on the container of dangerous chemical compounds to indicate the specific risk and thus the required precaution.

3. Treated Seeds

Labels are placed on the bags containing treated seeds which are about to be planted or sold. The warning advice states that on no account should such seeds be eaten in order to prevent food poisoning. Treated seeds are planted not eaten.

4. Veterinary Drugs

These indicate the necessary details that allow the drugs to be safely used and in the case of food animals, protect the human food chain from drugs residues. Drug label is usually placed by the drug manufacturer. Information found on the label include; active ingredients or established name of the drug, animal species and dose, duration of the therapy, mode of administration and withdrawal times.

5. Implements

Labels are placed in important areas of the implements to draw attention to potential safety hazards. The words danger, warning and caution are used on the implement safety labels.
